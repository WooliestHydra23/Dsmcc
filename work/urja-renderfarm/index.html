Enterprise Cloud Rendering

Product & GTM Support • Technical Communication • Stakeholder Coordination

01 / Context

The Project

Urja Renderfarm is an enterprise-grade cloud rendering platform designed for studios, VFX houses, and animation teams. The platform manages complex 3D rendering workloads across distributed infrastructure—a technically sophisticated product serving a specialized market.

My involvement centered on making this complexity accessible to customers, sales teams, and internal stakeholders. The focus: clear documentation, sales enablement, and GTM strategy to support product launch and market positioning.

02 / Problem

What Was Broken

Documentation Gap: Enterprise customers needed clear guides on platform capabilities, workflow integration, troubleshooting, and best practices. Without this, onboarding friction slowed adoption and support tickets multiplied.

Sales Enablement Mismatch: The sales team lacked clear, concise materials to explain technical differentiation to non-technical decision-makers (studio heads, VFX supervisors, IT leads).

Stakeholder Alignment: Product, sales, and support teams operated with different understandings of platform capabilities, messaging, and customer value propositions—creating handoff friction.

Knowledge Siloing: Critical product knowledge lived in code, internal conversations, and tribal memory. No single source of truth for customers or internal teams.

03 / My Role

What I Did

Documentation Architecture: Designed a structured FAQ and knowledge base framework organized by customer journey stage: pre-integration (capabilities, pricing), integration (API, workflows), production (performance tuning, troubleshooting), and support (edge cases, best practices).

Content Development: Wrote 30+ FAQ items, integration guides, and troubleshooting docs. Conducted product interviews to extract knowledge. Iterated with support team feedback to ensure accuracy and customer relevance.

Video & Tutorial Scripting: Outlined and scripted 8+ tutorial videos covering core workflows (job submission, monitoring, result retrieval, pipeline integration). Coordinated with product/demo team on technical accuracy and visual clarity.

Sales Enablement: Created one-pagers explaining technical value to different buyer personas (studio leads, VFX supervisors, IT). Developed comparison docs against competitive offerings. Built internal training deck for sales team onboarding.

Stakeholder Coordination: Facilitated product/sales/support alignment meetings. Synthesized feedback from all teams into documentation priorities and updates. Ensured consistency across all customer-facing materials.

04 / Process

How I Approached It

Discovery: Interviews with product team (capabilities, roadmap), sales team (objections, common questions), and support (recurring issues). Reviewed competitive products to understand positioning gaps.

Information Architecture: Mapped customer journey and decision points. Organized content by persona and use case rather than just feature lists. Created hierarchical structure (category → subtopic → FAQ/guide) for discoverability.

Content Development: Wrote in customer voice, not product voice. Tested explanations with non-expert readers to ensure clarity. Built iteratively—published core docs first, then expanded based on feedback.

Coordination: Weekly sync with product to validate accuracy. Monthly reviews with sales to measure material effectiveness. Quarterly updates with support to incorporate new issue patterns.

Quality Gates: Technical accuracy reviewed by engineering. Clarity reviewed by customer-facing teams. Consistency checked across all materials.

05 / Output

What Was Delivered

Documentation Suite: 30+ FAQ items (organized by topic), integration guides, API documentation, troubleshooting playbooks, best practices guides, video scripts, and training materials.

Sales Materials: Persona-specific one-pagers (Studio Lead, VFX Supervisor, IT Infrastructure), competitive comparison docs, internal sales training deck, objection handling guide.

Internal Knowledge: Process documentation for documentation updates, feedback loops between support/product/sales, and a system for incorporating new learnings into customer-facing materials.

Video Content: Scripts and outlines for 8+ tutorial videos covering core workflows, ready for demo/video production team implementation.

06 / Impact

What Changed

Reduced Onboarding Friction: New customers could self-serve answers to common questions, reducing support load and accelerating time-to-value.

Improved Sales Effectiveness: Sales team had clear materials to explain technical value to non-technical buyers. Support for objections and competitive positioning improved deal closure rates.

Aligned Stakeholders: Product, sales, and support operated from shared documentation. Handoff friction decreased. Cross-team communication improved.

Foundation for Scale: Documentation framework supports rapid onboarding of new team members and customers. Creates a scalable knowledge system as product grows.

08 / Reflection

What I Learned

Documentation is GTM: Clear documentation isn't a post-launch task—it's a critical part of go-to-market strategy. Good docs reduce friction, accelerate adoption, and improve customer satisfaction.

Systems Beat Content: A single great doc loses value if nobody maintains it. Building systems for feedback, updates, and knowledge capture beats writing one-off docs.

Bridge Internal Silos: The most valuable work happened at intersections—coordinating between product, sales, and support. Documentation became a shared language for alignment.

Know Your Personas: Writing FAQ for "enterprise customers" fails. Writing for "Studio Lead concerned about ROI" or "VFX Supervisor evaluating integration effort" succeeds. Understanding buyer motivation matters more than feature lists.
